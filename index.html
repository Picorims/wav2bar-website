The free, open source audio visualization creator.

Download

Wav2Bar lets you make nice visuals for your audio source, to then share on the internet.

Free

You will never have to pay to use Wav2Bar. Your projects remains yours, forever.

Open Source

Wav2Bar is MIT licensed, a pretty permissive lisense. That means for example you can browse the source code, and even modify it locally.

Customization

Every element has customization options to let you make something that suits you the most. One is missing ? Request it!

Video Export

Use whatever resolution you like, and export is as .mp4 for your website, your social medias, your blog post...

Step 1 - setup your project

Set the video framerate and resolution, and choose the desired audio source. You can save and load back your project at any time, and all the assets are copied into the save file, which makes it easy to transfer and backup.

Step 2 - get creative

Wav2Bar work with objects that you add to craft your video. These of course include visualizers, but also timers, text, images (that can also be used as basic rectangles and ellipses), particle flows and backgrounds.

Step 3 - export your work

Set the output path, press export, and wait for the export to finish. Once done, you will find a .mp4 video file in the directory you specified, ready to be uploaded online!

All kinds of features exists to make unique videos.

  • Save your projects, and use them as templates;
  • Multiple audio visualizer types (More to come);
  • Custom resolution and framerate;
  • Time indicators;
  • Images, like your own brand logo;
  • Particle flows;
  • And more...

Note: Wav2Bar is still in beta, therefore it is not exempt of bugs. Please backup your projects in case something goes wrong! If you find any bug (as small as it seems), please report it! If you have any suggestions for new features or improvements, you can also submit them here.

Current development focus:

Right now, I am mostly working on improving the user interface and how the user can interact with the application more easily. Some internal refactoring is also needed to make this easier. I also take a look at performances and have potential ideas to improve rendering performances.

Linux:

There are no Linux package available for download right now, but you can find configurations for .deb and .rpm packages in package.JSON. Thus if you have a Linux machine and install the required dependencies, you should be able to make them by yourself, however there is no guarantee they will work as I was never able to test them.

About the creator:

Hi, my name is Picorims, a freshman computer science student and the creator of Wav2Bar. The idea of Wav2Bar came a few months after the discontinuation of Sonic Candle. It was a similar tool that allowed you to make and export basic audio visualization. I always felt limited, and couldn't imagine the only open source option I knew and enjoyed would die. So, I tried my best to take what made Sonic Candle a great tool, and focused on the ability to add more customization. While being a project on my spare time, I still try to provide something as professional as possible. I am also open to suggestions and contributions.
